A flower is a plant's colorful reproductive structure, featuring parts like petals (attract pollinators), sepals (protect bud), sta mens (male pollen producers), and a pistil (female egg holder), designed to attract insects or birds for pollination, leading to seeds and fruit, and varies greatly in shape, color, and scent across thousands of species like roses, lilies, and orchids, existing as individual blooms or clusters called inflorescences.

ROSE

A rose is a woody perennial plant, known for its large, showy flowers with layered petals in colors like red, pink, white, yellow, and orange, often featuring a rich fragrance and typically thorny stems with glossy, serrated leaves, symbolizing love and beauty across cultures, with wild varieties having five petals and cultivated ones often having many more.

DAISY

Daisies are cheerful, classic flowers from the Asteraceae family, known for their star-shaped flower heads featuring bright yellow centers (disk florets) surrounded by white, pink, or other colored petals (ray florets). They symbolize innocence and purity, often growing in sunny meadows or gardens, with common types including the English Daisy (Bellis perennis) and Shasta Daisy (Leucanthemum Ã— superbum).

SAMPAGUITA

The Sampaguita (Jasminum sambac) is the national flower of the Philippines, a small, white, star-shaped bloom known for its intensely sweet fragrance, symbolizing purity, fidelity, and hope; it's a woody shrub or vine cultivated for garlands, perfumes, and religious offerings, often used in traditional leis and as an emblem of Filipino culture.

SANTAN

The Santan flower (scientific name Ixora), or Jungle Flame, is a vibrant tropical shrub known for its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers in bright colors like red, pink, orange, yellow, and white, blooming year-round on leathery, glossy leaves, making it a popular ornamental plant for hedges and gardens, often used by children for making necklaces.

SUN FLOWER

Sunflowers (Helianthus annuus) are tall, erect annuals with rough, hairy stems, large heart-shaped leaves, and distinctive, large flower heads (inflorescences) featuring bright yellow ray "petals" surrounding a central disk of small, spiraled disk florets that mature into edible seeds, often displaying intricate Fibonacci patterns. Known for following the sun (heliotropism in youth) and their iconic appearance, they are valued ornamentally and as a vital food crop, with cultivated types growing much larger than wild varieties.
